### Tweet 3 — Pain Point: Estimating Takes Too Long

The average cost estimate takes 4-8 hours of manual work.

Measuring plans. Looking up material prices. Cross-referencing labor rates by region. Formatting it into something a client can read.

Then the scope changes and you start over.

There has to be a faster way. (We think there is.)

### Tweet 4 — Pain Point: Estimating Costs Too Much

PlanSwift: $1,600/year.
Buildxact: $300/month.

And most of these tools still require hours of manual input on top of the subscription.

We built Takeoff.ai starting at $79/mo because estimating software shouldn't cost more than some of the jobs you're bidding on.

### Tweet 5 — Industry Insight

Something most estimating tools get wrong: they treat the whole country like one market.

Framing labor in rural Texas is not the same as framing labor in metro Boston. Materials fluctuate by region. Even permit costs vary by zip code.

Location-adjusted pricing isn't a feature. It's a requirement.
